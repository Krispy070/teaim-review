What I’d tighten next (quick wins)

Hierarchy/Contrast

KPI tiles: dark label, bright value. Right now the label/value contrast is close.

Timeline: lighten rails slightly; make active bars bolder so the eye locks onto progress.

Card headers: increase contrast and tighten vertical padding so cards read “denser, sharper”.

Color system

Use one accent (TEAIM orange) for primary CTAs only. The gold can be secondary emphasis (counts, highlights), not buttons.

Notifications, warnings, success: use tinted pills (transparent backgrounds) so they don’t compete with CTAs.

Spacing & density

Reduce inner card padding from 24 → 20px; header padding from 16 → 12px.

Button group (“Week/Month/Quarter/Export/Share”): tighten to 8px gaps and uniform widths.

Borders & radii

Keep a single radius system: 14px cards, 12px buttons, 10px pills.

Lower border contrast a hair to avoid “outlined everywhere” look.

Typography

Sidebar labels: use 12px/600 for section headers, 14px/500 for items.

KPI values: 20–22px/700, labels 12px/600.

Card titles: 15px/700, body 13.5–14px/500.

Icon legibility

All sidebar and header icons should use var(--text-soft) by default and var(--brand-orange) when active/hover.

Replace emoji buttons in “Team Wellness” with consistent vector icons (still fun, but crisp).

Header

Tighten to a single line with centered customer identity, keep implementor + env pill + bell + avatar on the right.

Consider dropping the “Customer” label word and just show the logo/name—less chrome, more brand.
